What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a site saves to your device to remember information about your visit. Some are essential for security and for things like keeping you signed in or remembering your language. Others help measure what works on the site, so we can fix issues and improve navigation. A few support personalization, such as showing relevant content based on general preferences, not on sensitive categories. Modern browsers let you inspect, block, or delete these files whenever you decide. In this document, you’ll see our brand referred to as spinmama and as spin mama for clarity.

How and why cookies are used

We use cookies to keep the site running smoothly, to understand what people find helpful, and to make features feel consistent between visits. Essential cookies handle things like session integrity and fraud prevention, while performance cookies tell us which pages load quickly or need attention. Functional cookies remember preferences such as language or accessibility options you’ve chosen. Advertising or measurement tools, where present, focus on broad trends and never guarantee results of any kind. You can control most of this behavior directly in your browser, and we’ll respect those settings across spin mama and experiences associated with spinmama. Below is a quick guide to common categories we may use in different parts of the product:

  • Essential: sign-in, page routing, session continuity, and fraud prevention.

  • Personalization: content or layout tuned to general interests you’ve shown.

  • Advertising/Measurement: aggregated reach and frequency, where applicable.
